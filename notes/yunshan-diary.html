Overview

Project Scope

The project centers on the text Yunshan Diary (云山日记) and related Yuan Jiangnan materials. Its aim is not simply to preserve diary text, but to convert that text into structured research data that can support historical, spatial, and network analysis.

Public Framing

This update presents the repository as a full DH research pipeline. The workflow now moves from source preservation and annotation to derived events, graph generation, semantic exports, publication-ready tables and figures, and Neo4j-based exploratory analysis.

Latest Update

Repository Expansion

Over the latest Codex work cycle, the Yunshan Diary repository was expanded from a text-extraction workspace into a layered DH pipeline. The current workflow now covers source-text preservation, machine extraction, human annotation, merged delivery records, derived event layers, knowledge-graph exports, semantic assertions, RAG chunks, JSON-LD/RDF outputs, and chapter-oriented analysis assets.

In practical terms, this means a diary entry can now remain anchored in its source text while also being transformed into structured metadata, analytical events, graph relations, publication figures, and Neo4j-ready network or spatial data.
